The Temporal Encoding Problem

Why the log-decay penalty is wrong, what double-counting means, and how to fix it — visually.

1 We Have Two Systems Fighting Each Other

Our vector search has two mechanisms that both try to control time. They work in completely different ways and they step on each other.

This is double-counting. The circular encoding already adds a time bonus/penalty through cos(λπ·Δt). Then hybrid subtracts MORE through log(1+Δt). Two mechanisms, one variable, both pushing the same direction. You can't tune them independently.

2 The Math, Side by Side

Circular Encoding (the good one)

score = γ·sem_sim + Σᵢ γᵢ·cos(λᵢπ·Δt)

γ + Σγᵢ = 1 → it's a convex combination. Score is always bounded in [-1, 1].
Time decays SMOOTHLY: cos starts at 1, glides down to 0 at Δt=0.5/λ, bottoms at -1.
Controllable: smaller λ = slower decay. λ=0.1 means the first zero-crossing is at 5 time units.

Hybrid Re-rank with Log (the broken one)

score = γ·sem_sim + Σᵢ γᵢ·cos(λᵢπ·Δt) − α·log(1 + Δt)

Three things wrong with the red part:
① Unbounded: as Δt → ∞, penalty → ∞, score → negative infinity. A 100-year-old document with perfect semantic match gets -∞ score.
② Wrong slope: penalty grows fastest in 0–60s range (where real conversations live), then goes flat everywhere else.
③ Duplicate: cos(λπ·Δt) already handles time. The penalty hits the same thing twice.

4 The FIX: Exponential Saturation

If we absolutely must have a penalty, use this. Move the λ slider to see how the knee shifts.

penalty = α · (1 − e−λ·Δt)

Bounded at α. Smooth everywhere. λ controls where it saturates.

■ Log (broken) — unbounded, wrong slope
■ Exponential — bounded, smooth, single knob (λ)
■ Hyperbolic — heavier tail, saturates slower
■ Tanh — sharpest knee, fastest saturation

5 The "Echo Problem" Was Wrong From the Start

The hybrid re-rank was invented to fix "cosine echoes at λ > 2." But this claim has two problems:

Problem 1: Echoes exist at EVERY λ — not just > 2

Cosine is periodic. The period is 2/λ. λ=1 echoes at Δt=2. λ=0.1 echoes at Δt=20. λ=5 echoes at Δt=0.4. There's nothing special about λ=2. The echo distance just gets shorter as λ increases.

cos(λπ·Δt) = 1 → Δt = 0, 2/λ, 4/λ, 6/λ, ...
cos(λπ·Δt) = 0 → Δt = 0.5/λ, 1.5/λ, 2.5/λ, ...
cos(λπ·Δt) = −1 → Δt = 1/λ, 3/λ, 5/λ, ...

The fix isn't a penalty function. The fix is picking the right λ. If your conversations span at most 100 time units, use λ ≤ 0.02 so the first echo is at Δt = 100 — safely beyond your range.

Problem 2: The penalty doesn't fix the echo ANYWAY

The log penalty shifts everything down by α·log(1+Δt). But the cosine still oscillates on top of it.

score = γ·sem_sim + Σᵢ γᵢ·cos(λᵢπ·Δt) − α·log(1+Δt)

At Δt = 2k/λ, the cosine term returns to +1 — the score gets a bump of +2γᵢ relative to the trough. The echo is still there, just riding on a lower baseline. Subtraction doesn't cancel oscillation.

■ Circular only — cosine oscillates, bounded
■ With log penalty — shifted down, still oscillating. Echo NOT fixed.

6 The Real Fix: Use Circular Encoding Alone

The clean system

score = γ·sem_sim + Σᵢ γᵢ·cos(λᵢπ·Δt)

That's it. One mechanism for time. No double-counting. No unbounded penalty. No fighting frameworks.

Verdict: Circular encoding alone is the answer. It's bounded, tunable, mathematically exact, and doesn't need a second penalty mechanism.

If you absolutely must have monotonic decay (no oscillations at all), use exponential saturation: α·(1 − e−λ·Δt) — but only if circular encoding's cosine doesn't meet your needs.
